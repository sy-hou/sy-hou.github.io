📐 Maths (8→9)

  • Focus on proof, vectors, harder algebra — that's where 9s are won
  • Do past paper questions by topic, not full papers, until Week 3
  • Every wrong answer: find the concept, re-do 3 similar questions
  • Use mark schemes actively — know the exact steps examiners want

✍️ English Language (6→7)

  • This is your biggest jump — treat it as top priority
  • Learn the 4-mark, 8-mark, 24-mark question structures by heart
  • Practise language analysis with specific terminology every session
  • Write at least one timed answer per week, mark against the mark scheme

🔬 Sciences (7-8→9)

  • Biology needs the most work — flashcard every definition
  • Required practicals come up every year — memorise all 6+ per subject
  • Maths in science loses easy marks — practise calculation questions
  • Use Cognito or FreeScienceLessons for quick video consolidation

🌍 Geography (7→8)

  • Case studies are everything — know 2-3 per topic cold
  • Practice 9-mark extended writing with the PEEL structure
  • Learn exact statistics for case studies (dates, figures, names)
  • Map skills and graphs trip people up — practise these specifically

💻 CS & French (maintain 9)

  • Don't neglect these just because you're predicted 9 — maintain momentum
  • CS: make sure you can write pseudocode and trace algorithms under pressure
  • French: do listening at least 3x per week — it deteriorates fastest
  • French writing: use complex structures and subjunctive to differentiate

⚡ General Technique

  • 25 min focus + 5 min break (Pomodoro) — do not skip breaks
  • Active recall beats re-reading every time — test yourself constantly
  • Sleep 8+ hrs the week of mocks — it affects performance more than revision
  • Never revise the same subject twice in a row in one session
